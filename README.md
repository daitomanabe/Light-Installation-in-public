# Lighthouse of Alexandria

Egypt, 299 BC

At its apex was positioned a mirror which reflected sunlight during the day; a fire was lit at night. It is said that it was able to transmit light for more than tens of kilometers.

# HELIOGRAPH

1899-1901

Heliograph (HELIO + GRAPH) is a Greek word meaning “sun writer”. Send the light of the sun by the mirror in Morse code to a distant place.  Heliograph was used for the first time by General De Wit in the Bohr War (1899-1902)

# FLIGHT COMMUNICATION

夜間のナビゲーションの発達は、戸外のかがり火に始まり、パイロットを援護する人工の光の合図に発展していった。この合図の閃光は連続して光り、固定されたタワーの光が次の広がりに誘導し、前にあったタワーにも光を１つ放つので空中に道路が出来上がる。正確に言うと、500ワットの回転するサーチライトが地上と平行して光線を放ちパイロットを誘導するが、その間別のサーチライトが空中に光を放って的確な道筋を教える。（ロジャー・モラ著『航空灯と電子ナビゲーション機器の発達』から引用）  

The development of navigation at night began as an outdoor beacon and evolved into an artificial light cue to help the pilot. The flash of this signal lights continuously, the light from the fixed tower guides to the next spread, and a road is created in the air as one light is also emitted to the tower which was in front. Roughly speaking, a 500 watts rotating searchlight emits light rays in parallel with the ground and guides the pilot, while another search light emits light in the air to teach a precise path. (Quoted from Roger Mora "Development of aircraft lights and electronic navigation equipment")

# Lindbergh Beacon

Los Angels, 1928

The Lindbergh Beacon, an aircraft beacon atop the Los Angeles City Hall, operated nightly from April 26, 1928, until just after the attack on Pearl Harbor. It was restored to its original condition in 2001. City officials now occasionally put the beacon into operation for special occasions such as the year-end holidays.

# Tribute in Light

John Bennett, Gustavo Boneverdi, Richard Nash Gould, Julian LaVerdiere, and Paul Myoda, with lighting consultant Paul Marantz.
